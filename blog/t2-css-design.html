Inline Vs. Block Vs. Inline-Block Display

By: Shahab Amin

4/21/15

Hello readers! On today's blog, I will explain the difference between Inline, Block, and Inline-block displays with regards to CSS.

Starting with Inline elements, they have a few properties. Inline elements:

  • Respect left and right margins and paddings, but not top or bottom.
  • Cannot have a width or height set.
  • Allow other elements to sit by their left and right.
An inline element has no line break before or after it, and tolerates HTML elements next to it.

Block Elements:

  • Respect all margins and paddings.
  • Force a line break after the block element.
Block element's have some whitespace above and below them and they do not tolerate any HTML elements next to the.

Inline-block elements:

  • Allow other elements to sit to their left and right.
  • Respect top and bottom margins and paddings, but not left or right.
  • Respect height and width.
